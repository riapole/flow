The Purpose

I have always been fascinated by understanding why people behave the way they do, how different environments shape individuals, and how genetics and environment influence the brain, behavior, and human experience.

This led to the creation of Flow — a space to explore questions surrounding the meaning and purpose of life, consciousness, wisdom, and the human condition through psychology, neuroscience, literature, art, and philosophy.

Why “Flow”?

“State of Flow,” a concept introduced by psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi, refers to a state of complete immersion in an activity under the right conditions.

During flow, individuals may experience time slowing down, distractions fading away, heightened attention, loss of self-consciousness, effortless action, and a deep synchronization between mind and body. The activity itself becomes intrinsically rewarding.

Before learning about this concept, I experienced this state personally without knowing it had a name.

Later, discovering the psychology behind flow inspired me to create this project and name it “Flow.”
