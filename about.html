The Purpose

I have always been fascinated by understanding why people behave the way they do based on different environments, how the environment and genetics influence the brain and behavior and vice versa.

This lead to the purpose of creating a space to explore matters such as the meaning and purpose of life, the wisdom of life, consciousness, and human experience through psychology, neuroscience, literature, art, and philosophy.

Why name it “Flow”?

"State of Flow,” named by psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi, refers to a state of being where an individual immerses in an activity in such a way under the right conditions that they experience elements such as time slowing down, losing oneself completely in an activity, sync of body and mind, distractions fading away, acting instinctively and naturally, self-consciousness disappearing, activity itself becoming a reward, attention sharpening, and self-awareness dissolving.

I got to experience this state of being numerous times, which at the time I did not know existed. It was a life-changing moment of meeting with my purest authentic self. It was liberating and awakening. It changed my perception of everything around me, including myself. Later, I got to learn about this concept, which inspired me to name this project “Flow”. Beyond just this experience, the desire to effortlessly keep growing, experiencing, learning and moving forward, without being stagnant just like flowing water, also connects back to the word "flow".

These experiences inspire me to chase these abstracts, rather questions, about the meaning of life, happiness, the righteous way to make the most out of this life, purpose, consciousness and furthermore. As best put by writer E. B. White,"If the world were merely seductive, that would be easy. If it were merely challenging, that would be no problem. But I arise in the morning torn between a desire to improve the world and a desire to enjoy the world. This makes it hard to plan the day.” It is fascinating how he captured this universal tension between wanting to care selflessly about the well-being of others and the pursuit of pleasure, between wanting to fix the world’s problems and wanting simply to sit back and capture the beauty of life. If it were simply about pleasure, the only struggle would be to exercise self-control; if it were simply about challenges, the only challenge would be to do what needs to be done. But the burden of waking up and making this decision every single day between two completely valid desires pulling you in opposite directions reminds us to find a balance between them.

Thus, this project is more about keeping consistency with the "desire to enjoy the world" part of my life. It is a way to keep me going, to keep looking out for the answers to my questions and to deepen these questions further, to keep reflecting on them and keep records of these reflections, and to broaden the knowledge endlessly. It would also be insightful to hear other people's opinions based on their lived experiences. I believe no single thing is set in stone or has an objective meaning. Everything can be re-invented or interpreted through our own subjective viewpoints. Life and reality are fluid constructs. I believe there is no single "right or wrong." Every single event or truth is experienced by everyone through a very unique lens based on their biases and experiences. Thus, truth is just a compilation of millions of different interpretations, which makes it essential to learn and further broaden the understanding of the world by listening to the lived experiences of people of different backgrounds and experiences.
